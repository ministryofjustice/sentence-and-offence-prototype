Identifying relevant remandRound 1

What we did

We designed a journey where users could select an adjustment type from a list and then continue on the journey to add adjustment type specific data then potentially continue on to add further adjustments or confirm and leave the service.

For this round we focused on the remand journey and the introduction of the relevant remand calculation tool, we wanted to validate our assumptions of the service and gain insights into the data the users need to be able to confidently see if the calculations were accurate and if they would trust the service.

What users said

  • 3/5 participants need to see relationships between the cases. Suggest that remand details compressed down so that it is clear why the total remand days is what it is. Could be confusing when it looks like he is getting two periods of 6 days and the total is 11.
  • 2/5 users would expect to see an audit trail or sentence history - allow them to see how remand dates have been arrived at and allow them to see who has added remand previously so that they could ask them if they had questions.
  • 2/5 participants needed to see offence date to support them in linking the periods of remand on the service with court documents and Nomis

Design recommendations

  • Add offence outcomes
  • Add offence dates
  • Explore ways to show the relationships between cases in case breakdown
